Introduction¶

This project presents a detailed walkthrough, of the exploratory analysis of a movie dataset, to gain meaningful insights, and draw conclusions from the data.

Dataset Description¶

This data set contains information about 10,000 movies collected from The Movie Database (TMDb), including user ratings and revenue. TMDb dataset contained 21 columns which are as follows: id
imdb_id, popularity, budget, revenue, original_title, cast, homepage, director, tagline, keywords , overview , runtime, genres, production_companies, release_date, vote_count, vote_average, release_year, revenue_adj Through this dataset, I intend to answer some questions:

  • What relationship exists among columns of the dataset
  • What Genres of movies are popular?
  • What are the properties of high grossing movies?
    • Is there a Relationship between high revenue movies and budget, popularity, or release_year
  • What are the Trends in the number of movies released each year?
  • Are movies with a high budget popular?

Conclusions¶

In the final analysis, there is a positive correlation between the revenue of a movie, and its budget. The higher the budget of a movie, the higher the revenue.Also, the bigger the budget of a movie, the higher the popularity. Movies with a high popularity brought in a high revenue as seen from the dataset. The Adventure genres was the most popular genre recorded over the years.
